1. ANTI-AMNESIA SYSTEM (4-Layer Memory Stack)

This alone has a major impact.
Problem: after ~200K tokens (now 1M in some cases), your agent wakes up with amnesia. Every decision, every rejection, every strategic shift. Gone.
What we built:
Layer 1: Hourly Memory Summarizer. A cron runs every hour and distills conversations into structured daily memory files. Not transcripts. Distillations. Decisions, action items, rejected ideas.
Layer 2: Post-Compaction Context Injector. When the platform compacts (and it will), we detect it and inject the last 24 hours of summaries, 15 recent user messages, 15 assistant messages, 10 system messages, and 15 thinking blocks. The agent wakes up from compaction and doesn't notice it happened.
Layer 3: Vector Memory Pipeline. FAISS index with sentence-transformers. 377+ conversation chunks embedded. Semantic search in under 300ms. The agent searches its own memory like Google searches the web.
Layer 4: Semantic Recall Hook. Every prompt auto-triggers memory search. Relevant past conversations surface before the agent even starts thinking.
Result: Zero noticeable knowledge loss across 8 weeks of continuous operation.
I discovered the amnesia problem the hard way. Week 1, I spent 45 minutes giving Oracle detailed context about a client's SEO strategy. Competitive landscape, keyword priorities, content gaps. The next morning I asked Oracle to build on that analysis. Blank stare. "I don't have any context about that client." 45 minutes of strategic thinking, gone. That's when I realized: the agents aren't the product. The memory is.

2. CROSS-AGENT SHARED BRAIN (Not a Database. A Filesystem.)

Most "multi-agent" setups are isolated chatbots that never talk to each other.
We have 4 agents. They share one brain:
shared-context/
├── priorities.md          ← all agents read this
├── kpis/current.md        ← everyone knows the numbers
├── feedback/              ← rejections teach ALL agents
├── agent-outputs/         ← Flash sees Oracle's work
├── signals.json           ← cross-agent signal bus
└── channel-workflows.yaml ← routing rules
The signal bus is the breakthrough most people miss.
When Oracle finds a high-converting keyword, it writes a signal. Flash reads it on its next run and creates content targeting that keyword. No human routing. No copy-pasting between chat windows.
When I reject a content angle, the rejection reason flows to ALL agents. Oracle stops recommending SEO pages on that topic. Flash stops pitching content on it. Cyborg adjusts the recruiting brief if it was a role-related rejection.
This is a symlinked directory. Not a vector database. Not an API. A filesystem. It works because every agent reads from shared-context/ on every run. Dead simple. Zero infrastructure cost.

5. THE ELON ALGORITHM (Self-Auditing System)

Most agent builders keep adding. We run a monthly audit that forces deletion.
The 5 steps:
  1. QUESTION. Who asked for this cron? Does it connect to revenue within 2 steps? Has Eric ever acted on its output?
  2. DELETE. If nobody acts on it for 3 weeks, kill it.
  3. SIMPLIFY. We consolidated 61 crons down to a target of 8-12.
  4. ACCELERATE. Auto-execute proven actions (low-risk items skip approval).
  5. AUTOMATE. Close the loop: outreach → reply → meeting → revenue.
Result from the first audit:
Killed: strategic digests, self-improvement crons, competitive intel nobody read, calendar scoring, 3 infrastructure crons burning $37/week on the wrong model. Consolidated: 3 outbound crons into 1. 2 SEO crons into 1. 3 intel crons into 1. Discovered: 60% of our AI spend was on 3 infrastructure crons that should have been on a cheaper model.
The uncomfortable math: we were spending more on AI maintenance than on the AI doing actual work.
The one that hurt the most to kill was the competitive intel digest. I'd built it in week 3 and was proud of it. Every morning, a beautifully formatted summary of what our competitors were doing. New blog posts, pricing changes, social media moves. It felt productive to read. But the audit asked the question: "Has Eric ever taken a specific action based on this cron's output?" The answer was no. Not once in 5 weeks. I was reading it like a morning newspaper. Interesting but not actionable. Killed it. Saved $12/week in API costs and 15 minutes of my morning. Multiply that across the 20+ crons we killed, and we recovered almost 3 hours of daily attention.

6. WEEKLY METRICS ENFORCEMENT (CEO Dashboard in Telegram)

I have 8 numbers I need to see every Monday: Net Revenue Retention, team utilization, margin, churn (by business unit), pipeline velocity, and content performance.
The old way: ask someone to pull a report. Wait. Get a spreadsheet. Squint.
The new way: a cron pulls from ClickFlow (Stripe API), HubSpot, Google Sheets, Gong, Mixpanel, and GA4. Computes week-over-week deltas. Flags anything off-target. Arrives Monday 7am as a single Telegram message with red/green indicators.
The key insight: it compares against 3HAG (3-year highly achievable goal) targets, not just last week. So it's not "revenue went up 3%." It's "revenue is 12% below the trajectory needed to hit your 3-year target."
That reframe changes how you feel about a "good week."
